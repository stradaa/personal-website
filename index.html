Professional

Research Direction

I am a PhD candidate in Bioengineering at the University of Pennsylvania in Bijan Pesaran's lab. My research focuses on the neural basis of movement and the development of technologies that can support reliable motor brain-computer interfaces.

My current work combines high-density micro-electrocorticography arrays with intracortical Neuropixels recordings in non-human primates to study how motor-related signals are organized across cortical surface maps, depth, and time.
